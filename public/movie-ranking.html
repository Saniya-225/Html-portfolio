TOP BEST MOVIES ACCORDING TO SANIYA

My Top 3 movies/series of all time

Jujutsu Kaisen

Jujutsu Kaisen is a favorite for many because it combines thrilling action, deep emotions, and stunning visuals all in one. The story follows Yuji Itadori, an ordinary boy who enters the world of curses after swallowing a powerful cursed object. What makes the anime stand out is its unique power system of cursed energy, creative techniques like Domain Expansions, and unforgettable characters such as Gojo Satoru, who bring both strength and charm to the series. Beyond the fights, it explores themes of life, death, and human emotions in a way that feels relatable and impactful. With its unpredictable twists, emotional storytelling, and breathtaking animation, Jujutsu Kaisen has captured the hearts of fans worldwide, making it one of the most beloved modern anime.

Weak hero class 1

The drama is a favorite because it’s not just about fights; it explores friendship, loyalty, trauma, and the harsh realities of bullying in schools. The acting, especially by Park Ji-hoon as Si-eun, is powerful and layered, showing how a “weak-looking” boy can be one of the strongest. The realistic storytelling, emotional impact, and gripping action scenes make it stand out among youth dramas.

John Wick

The John Wick movies are considered very good because they bring something fresh to the action genre. The fight scenes are not just random explosions and punches—they’re beautifully choreographed, almost like a dance, blending martial arts, gunplay, and stunts in a way that feels intense and realistic. The world-building also makes it unique, with the mysterious assassin society, secret rules, and the Continental Hotel adding depth beyond just action. At the heart of it, John Wick isn’t just a killer—he’s a man grieving his wife and fighting for the last memory of her, which makes the story emotional as well as thrilling. Keanu Reeves’ performance, combined with stylish cinematography, powerful music, and nonstop action, makes the series stand out and keeps audiences hooked.
